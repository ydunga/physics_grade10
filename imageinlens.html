Image formation in lenses

Just like in mirrors, the image formed after refraction of light depends on

1. distance of the object from the lens

2. the focal length of the lens

To figure out the position, size and nature of the image we can follow the steps below.

1. Draw a ray parallel to the principal axis. After refracting through the lens, the ray has to pass through the principal focus.

2. Draw a light ray passing through the principal focus of the lens. After passing through the lens the ray will be parallel to the principal axis.

3. Finally, draw a light ray passing through the centre of curvature. This ray will will not be refracted.

The image will be formed where two rays of light meet.

Power of a lens

The power of a lens is the extent to which a lens can refract income light waves.

A lens with a short focal length, will cause larger refraction of a light ray.

We can express the power of a lens as the reciprocal of the focal length of a lens.

The unit of measurement of a lens is dioptre (D). If the focal length is expressed in meters, then the power of the lens is expressed in dioptres.

If you're given a lens of power -2.5D, this means the lens is concave and it has a focal length of 1/(-2.5) = -0.4m.
