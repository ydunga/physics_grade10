Magnification

Depending on the position of the object and the focal length of the mirror, the size of the image in the reflection can be larger or smaller than the object itself.

The extent to which the image of an object is smaller or larger than the object itself is known as magnification.

If the height of the object is h and the height of the image of the object is h' then magnification(m) = h'/h.

The longer the focal length of a mirror, the higher the magnification of the image.

Before we go in to the formula for magnification , it is first useful to lay down some convention about how we measure things like distance of the object from the pole, the focal length etc.

When talking about spherical mirrors, we always plce the object of discussion to the left of the mirror.

The pole is considered the origin. So everything else is measured with respect to the pole.

Distance to the right of the pole is measured as +ve, while distance to the left of the origin is taken as -ve.

Distance above the origin is taken as +ve, while distance below the origin is taken as -ve.

Now that you know the convention, you can think about measuring the magnification power of a concave or a convex mirror.

The distance of the object from the pole is represented by u.

The distance of the reflected image from the pole is represented as v and the focal length is f.
