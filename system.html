System of resistors

Now that you know what a resistor is, maybe you can start to understand the importance they play in controlling current across a circuit.

When arranging resistors in a circuit, we can take two main approaches:

1. Series

2. Parallel

Resistors in a series

In the diagram below, you can see three resistors have been connected to each other.

When connected in a series, the resistance of the combined resistors is equal to the sum of each individual resistor.

In contrast to the diagram above, the resistors in the circuit shown below are connected to each other in a parallel fashion.

One of the disadvantages of a series of resistors is that when one component of the circuit fails, the whole whole circuit stops working. In a circuit with parallel resistors, the current is divided throughout the circuit so the failure of one component does not mean the whole device stops working.
