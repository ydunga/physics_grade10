Magnets and electric current

How do you think a current wire and a magnet would react when placed in close proximity to each other?

Below you have a set up, where you are showing a regular horesehoe magnet and an aluminium rod.

For the purpose of explanation, assume that the rod is connected to a circuit and has a current passing through it.

*click on the rod to see what happens!

If the current through the rod is passing from point A to B and the rod is placed in the middle of the magnet, then the "A" end of the rod will shift to the left.

The displacement of the aluminium rod suggests there is a force acting on it.

If we were to reverse the direction of current through the circuit then the reverse displacement would be observed.

Fleming's left hand rule

Experiment have shown that the displacement of the aluminium rod is maximum when direction of current through the circuit is at a right angle to the direction of the magnetic field.

If we assume that the magnetic field and the direction of the current are indeed at 90 degree angle to each other, then the direction of the force applied on the conductor can be found using fleming's left hand rule.

You can use your left hand to easily figure out the direction of force on a conductor.


Common devices that use principles of electromagnetism include the electric motor, fans, loudspeakers and generators.
